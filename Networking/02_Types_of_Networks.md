# Types of Networks

## Definition

Networks are classified based on the geographical area they cover and the number of devices they connect.

The five main types are:

- PAN (Personal Area Network)
- LAN (Local Area Network)
- CAN (Campus Area Network)
- MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
- WAN (Wide Area Network)

---

## Why

Different situations require different network sizes.

- **PAN** – Personal devices
- **LAN** – Home or office
- **CAN** – Campus or organization
- **MAN** – Entire city
- **WAN** – Countries or worldwide

---

## Working

As the coverage area increases, the network size also increases.

```text
PAN → LAN → CAN → MAN → WAN

(Smallest → Largest)
```

---

## Real-World Example

- **PAN** – Mobile connected to Bluetooth earbuds.
- **LAN** – Home Wi-Fi connecting a laptop, phone, and TV.
- **CAN** – University campus connecting multiple buildings.
- **MAN** – A bank connecting all its branches within a city.
- **WAN** – The Internet connecting computers around the world.

---

## Cybersecurity Perspective

- **PAN** – Secure Bluetooth communication to prevent wireless attacks.
- **LAN** – Protect against malware spread, ARP spoofing, and unauthorized devices.
- **CAN** – Use VLANs and network segmentation to stop lateral movement.
- **MAN** – Encrypt communication between city-wide locations.
- **WAN** – Protect against DDoS, IP spoofing, and remote attacks using VPNs and firewalls.

**Answer:** PAN

### Q2. Which network is commonly used in homes?

**Answer:** LAN

### Q3. Which network connects multiple buildings in a university?

**Answer:** CAN

**Answer:** MAN
